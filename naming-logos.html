Strong candidates with astro/star roots, paired taglines, and logo concepts built on the Midnight Violet identity system.

01 — Name Candidates

The Shortlist

Every name here has a clear star/astro connection so users instantly get the category. Prioritizing short, feminine-leaning, brandable words that don't collide with existing products.

★ Strong Pick
Vela
/VEE-lah/
◆ Latin: "sails" — the constellation of the great ship Argo Navis

Why it hits: A real constellation name, but one almost nobody uses in branding. Four letters, two syllables, instantly memorable. The "sails" meaning is poetic — you're navigating by the stars, sailing toward the right place. Feels feminine and adventurous without being precious. It has that Co-Star quality of being a real word used in a surprising context. Incredibly easy to say, spell, and search for.

Paired Taglines
"Navigate by starlight."
"Your stars. Your map. Your move."
"Set sail by the stars."
✓ Real constellation — authentic ✓ 4 letters, dead simple ✓ @vela.app likely available ✓ Sails = journey = perfect metaphor ⚡ Check domain — vela.app or getvela.com
★ Strong Pick
Paran
/PAIR-an/
◆ From "paran lines" — advanced astrocartography planetary crossing points

Why it hits: This is an insider term that signals deep expertise. Paran lines are where two planetary influences cross on a map — the most powerful spots in astrocartography. Astrology-educated users will immediately respect the name. For newcomers, it sounds cool, short, and mysterious. Five letters. Completely unique in the app space. It's the technical term turned brand — like how Figma is a term from sculpture, or how Notion is a simple concept word elevated. Helena Woods and NJ Kaiulani would love that you named it after a real astrocartography concept.

Paired Taglines
"Where your stars cross."
"Find where you converge."
"Every place on your map means something."
✓ Deep astrocartography credibility ✓ Zero brand conflicts ✓ 5 letters, easy to say/spell ✓ Insider term → instant respect ⚡ Less intuitive for total newcomers
Elara
/eh-LAR-ah/
◆ One of Jupiter's moons — also a Greek mythological figure loved by Zeus

Why it hits: Jupiter in astrocartography represents growth, luck, and expansion — exactly what people hope to find when they move to a new city. Naming the app after a Jupiter moon subtly channels that energy. Elara sounds elegant, mythological, and name-like — "Ask Elara where to move." Beautiful feminine ending. The Greek mythology root gives it depth without being obvious. Five letters, three syllables, flows beautifully.

Paired Taglines
"Discover where you expand."
"Your map is written in the stars."
"Go where you grow."
✓ Jupiter = growth (perfect theme) ✓ Elegant, mythological ✓ Feminine, name-like quality ⚡ Some minor uses (Elara Technologies) ⚡ Sounds similar to Alara, Elora
★ Strong Pick
Astra
/AS-trah/
◆ Latin: "stars" — as in per aspera ad astra, "through hardship to the stars"

Why it hits: The most direct star-connection possible. Five letters, two syllables, instantly says "this is about stars." Feminine, strong, and universally understood. "Per aspera ad astra" is tattooed on half of TikTok — the cultural resonance is already there. It works as a character name ("What does Astra say about Tokyo?") and as a brand. It's the kind of name where someone hears it once and never forgets it. The challenge is that Astra has some existing uses (the rocket company went through bankruptcy, AstraZeneca is in pharma), but in the astrology app space specifically, it's wide open.

Paired Taglines
"Through the stars, to your place."
"Your stars know where you belong."
"Find your place in the stars."
✓ Most direct star connection ✓ Cultural resonance (per aspera ad astra) ✓ Instantly understood ⚡ Astra rocket company (bankrupt, fading) ⚡ Domain will need creative TLD
Sidra
/SID-rah/
◆ From Latin "sideralis" (of the stars) — root of "sidereal" time in astrology

Why it hits: Sidereal time is what all astrological calculations are based on — it's the clock of the stars. "Sidra" extracts that root into a short, punchy, beautiful name. Sounds like a person's name (approachable) but has deep celestial etymology. Nobody is using this. Completely ownable. It avoids the "astro-" prefix that makes names feel literal, while still rooting in star-language. Five letters, clean, modern, works globally.

Paired Taglines
"Aligned by the stars."
"Not every city is your city."
"Your star map, decoded."
✓ Deep astro etymology ✓ Completely unique — zero conflicts ✓ 5 letters, globally pronounceable ⚡ Star connection not obvious to casual users
Astrid
/AS-trid/
◆ Old Norse: "divinely beautiful" — also echoes "astra" (stars)

Why it hits: A real name that starts with "Astr-" — immediately reads as star-related. Feels like a character, a guide ("Astrid says you'd thrive in Barcelona"). The Norse origin adds a sense of strength and adventure — think Nordic explorer energy. Less common in branding than you'd expect. It has the approachability of a name you've heard before but the star-connection makes it purposeful. Six letters, very easy to spell and pronounce.

Paired Taglines
"She knows where you shine."
"Your celestial compass."
"Star-guided, map-ready."
✓ Astr- prefix = instant star read ✓ Character-like, approachable ✓ Easy to spell and say ⚡ It's a human name — some overlap ⚡ How to Train Your Dragon character
Solara
/soh-LAR-ah/
◆ From Latin "sol" (sun) + "-ara" feminine ending — "of the sun"

Why it hits: The Sun is the most important body in a natal chart and in astrocartography — your Sun lines represent where you feel most "you." Solara captures that solar energy with a warm, feminine sound. It has a travel vibe — sounds like a beautiful Mediterranean town. The "-ara" ending matches the Stellara approach from the previous round but without the video game conflict. Six letters, three syllables, rolls off the tongue.

Paired Taglines
"Find where you shine."
"Bright places for bright people."
"Your light, mapped."
✓ Sun = core identity in astrology ✓ Warm, travel-feeling ✓ Feminine, elegant flow ⚡ Solara has some energy company uses ⚡ More sun than star — may feel less astro
Stellavie
/STELL-ah-vee/
◆ "Stella" (star) + "vie" (French: life/way) — "the star's path"

Why it hits: If you want to keep the "stella/star" energy from Stellaris but make it completely your own. Stellavie sounds like a French luxury brand — premium, feminine, sophisticated. "The way of the stars" is literally what astrocartography is. Three syllables, distinctive, would own search results completely. The French touch gives it the same elevated feeling that made "Atelier" and "Maison" fixtures in premium branding.

Paired Taglines
"Follow the star's path."
"Your stars. Your way."
"Life, directed by the stars."
✓ Keeps stella/star root ✓ Zero conflicts — completely coined ✓ Sounds premium/French luxury ⚡ 3 syllables — slightly longer ⚡ Spelling from hearing is tricky

Visual Identity Directions

All built on the Midnight Violet palette from the branding plan. Showing concepts for the top three names — each with a wordmark + symbol approach.

Vela — Constellation Sail

Minimal constellation-sail symbol connecting three stars in the Vela constellation shape. The triangle suggests a sail, journey, direction. Pairs with a light-weight serif wordmark.

App Icon

Constellation sail on deep navy. Works at 16×16 and 1024×1024. The glowing top star is the focal point.

Compact Lockup

Paran — Crossing Lines

Primary Wordmark + Crossing Lines Mark

Two planetary lines crossing at a glowing paran point — the exact astrological concept the name comes from. The radiating circles suggest energy at the intersection. Scientific precision meets celestial beauty.

App Icon

Two lines crossing with a glowing intersection. Feels like looking at a map — exactly right for astrocartography.

Compact Lockup

The crossing-lines mark scales down beautifully — still readable at small sizes.

Primary Wordmark + Star Compass Mark

A four-point star that doubles as a compass rose — stars meet navigation in one symbol. The four points map to the four astrocartography angles: MC, IC, ASC, DSC. Elegant, minimal, deeply meaningful. The center glow represents "you" at the center of your map.

App Icon

Star compass on deep navy. The glow at center makes it feel alive — like a star pulsing on your home screen.

Compact Lockup

The star compass reduces cleanly. Works in navigation bars, social bios, watermarks.

04 — My Take

Ranking the Candidates

Rank Name Star Signal Brandability Why
1 Vela ★★★★★ ★★★★★ 4 letters, real constellation, sail = journey. Perfect balance of immediate star recognition and unique brandability.
2 Paran ★★★★ ★★★★★ Strongest insider credibility. The astrocartography community would love it. Less obvious to total newcomers.
3 Astra ★★★★★ ★★★★ Most instantly understood star-name. Cultural resonance is huge. Domain/handle availability is the main risk.
4 Elara ★★★★ ★★★★ Beautiful, Jupiter-moon theme is perfect. Less immediately "star-sounding" to casual users.
5 Sidra ★★★ ★★★★★ Deepest etymology, completely ownable. Star-connection requires knowledge of "sidereal."
6 Astrid ★★★★ ★★★ Strong name-as-character angle. The "it's a human name" factor cuts both ways.
7 Solara ★★★★ ★★★ Warm and travel-y. Some energy company conflicts. "Sol" = sun, not stars — slight mismatch.
8 Stellavie ★★★★★ ★★★ Premium French luxury feel. 3 syllables and spelling-from-hearing are the downsides.

My honest take: Vela is the strongest all-around pick — it's a real constellation, it's four letters, it sounds beautiful, and the "sails" meaning is a perfect metaphor for an app about navigating by the stars. Paran is the boldest pick — it would earn instant respect from the astrocartography community and give Helena Woods something to genuinely love. Astra is the safest pick — everyone immediately gets "stars" — but domain/handle availability will make or break it.
